SPHERE LAB

Realistic 2D ball physics sandbox

Tools

Spawn Click to create a ball. Drag to slingshot. Shift-click for a burst of 10. Grab Pick up and fling balls. Velocity = how fast you dragged. Draw Click-drag to draw a static wall segment. Erase Remove balls, walls, or links near the cursor. Link Click two balls to connect them with a spring. Pin Click a ball to toggle whether it's fixed in place. Push Hold to push balls radially away from the cursor. Heat Paint heat onto balls — changes color, elasticity, friction.

Controls

Right-click Explosion force at cursor. Middle-drag Pan the camera. Wheel Zoom in/out (at cursor). ← → Actuate pinball flippers (in PINBALL scene). 1-9 Select a material. Q W E R T Y U I Select a tool. Space F G C Pause · Slow-mo · Gravity · Clear. B V S H M Bloom · Vectors · Sound · Heat · Motion-blur. Ctrl-R / 0 Reset scene · Reset camera.

Physics model

240 Hz fixed-timestep simulation with continuous collision detection. Impulse-based contacts with separate normal + tangential passes; tangential friction transfers linear momentum into spin. Spring + pendulum constraints resolve iteratively. Materials carry density, restitution, friction, metallic, glow, refraction, pitch, and timbre — heat modifies elasticity and friction per material. Buoyancy follows Archimedes, Magnus curves spinning trajectories, and a uniform spatial hash keeps broadphase O(n) on average.

Rendering

Gradient sphere shading with a metallic branch (env reflection bands) and a diffuse branch. Glass balls refract the current scene snapshot through an inverse lens. Half-resolution bloom buffer, chromatic aberration, film grain, fake ambient occlusion in contact gaps, motion streaks, and lens flares on glowing sources. Ground shadows only when gravity is on.
