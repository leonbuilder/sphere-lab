SPHERE LAB

Realistic 2D ball physics sandbox

Tools

Spawn Click to create a ball. Drag to slingshot. Shift-click for a burst of 10. Grab Pick up and fling balls. Velocity = how fast you dragged. Draw Click-drag to draw a static wall segment. Erase Remove balls, walls, or links near the cursor. Link Click two balls to connect them with a spring. Re-link same pair for reinforced strands. Drag through many balls to chain-link them all in one stroke. Ctrl+click a ball to auto-mesh — link it to every nearby ball in one click (drag outward to grow the radius). Shift+click target to keep source selected for rapid spoke-linking. Pin Click a ball to toggle whether it's fixed in place. Push Hold to push balls radially away from the cursor. Attract Hold to pull balls toward the cursor. Heat Paint heat onto balls — changes color, elasticity, friction.

Controls

Right-click Explosion force at cursor. Middle-drag Pan the camera. Wheel Zoom in/out (at cursor). ← → Actuate pinball flippers (in PINBALL scene). Ctrl-Z Undo last spawn / wall / link. 1-9 Select a material. Q W E R T Y U I O Select a tool. Space F G C Pause · Slow-mo · Gravity · Clear. B V S H M Bloom · Vectors · Sound · Heat · Motion-blur. Ctrl-R / 0 Reset scene · Reset camera.

Physics model

240 Hz fixed-timestep simulation with continuous collision detection. Impulse-based contacts with separate normal + tangential passes; tangential friction transfers linear momentum into spin. Spring + pendulum constraints resolve iteratively. Materials carry density, restitution, friction, metallic, glow, refraction, pitch, and timbre — heat modifies elasticity and friction per material. Buoyancy follows Archimedes, Magnus curves spinning trajectories, conveyor belts drag tangentially, and a uniform spatial hash keeps broadphase O(n) on average.

Rendering

Gradient sphere shading with a metallic branch (env reflection bands) and a diffuse branch. Glass balls refract the current scene snapshot through an inverse lens. Half-resolution bloom buffer, chromatic aberration, film grain, fake ambient occlusion in contact gaps, motion streaks, lens flares on glowing sources, and expanding impact rings on high-energy collisions.
